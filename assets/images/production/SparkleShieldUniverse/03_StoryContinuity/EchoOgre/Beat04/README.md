# EO-B04 - The First Echo

Book: The Echo Ogre
Spread: 04
Pages: 12-13
Status: prompt
Canonical: approved story board

## Story Text
Cash shouted. The street answered.

## Story Purpose
Show the catalyst: one impulsive shout travels through the Sparkle Grid and starts changing the environment.

## Story Function
Catalyst: Captain Cash shouts, a pulse travels through the Sparkle Grid, lights flicker, flowers close, music stops, and purple geometric fragments appear. Nobody notices except readers.

## Emotional Objective
Playful rivalry snaps into surprise.

## World Building
The Sparkle Grid carries emotional resonance through paths, lights, murals, flowers, and music systems.

## Page-Turn Hook
Purple pieces clicked open where no one was looking.

## Illustration Objective
A child's outburst causes the neighborhood itself to react.

Keep the existing narrative action: Create the catalyst spread. Captain Cash, in the middle of a brotherly disagreement, shouts impulsively. Show a visible emotional pulse leaving his body and traveling through the Sparkle Grid embedded in the sidewalk. The pulse should affect multiple systems at once: lights flicker, flowers close, music notes from wind sculptures stop midair, and small purple geometric fragments appear near the path and mural. Bubble Boy reacts with surprise, not blame. Nobody in the scene fully understands the danger yet, but readers should. Use Captain Cash expression references for impulsive frustration, the Environmental State Guide for first disturbance, and the Neighborhood Green/continuity maps for world accuracy.
