# Beat10 - When the Neighborhood Forgot

Use this folder for prompt notes, thumbnails, revisions, and finished spread art for Spread 10.

## Production Specification

**Series:** Sparkle Shield Bros  
**Book:** The Echo Ogre  
**Spread:** 10  
**Pages:** 24-25  
**Scene name:** When the Neighborhood Forgot  
**Story beat:** All Is Lost

## Story Purpose

By Beat 10, the story stops behaving like a superhero story and begins behaving like a psychological fairy tale. The Echo Ogre is not the villain; he is the visible consequence of emotional imbalance.

This beat should not show the Ogre getting stronger. It should show that **the neighborhood forgets itself**.

The brothers realize they misunderstood the lesson. Kindness is not a magical spell. It is a way of living. One moment of compassion paused the Echo Ogre, but the neighborhood cannot heal through one isolated act.

## Manuscript

The music faded again.

The flowers lowered their heads.

Even the stories in the mural forgot how they ended.

Bubble Boy whispered, "We didn't fix it."

## Story Function

This is the emotional low point.

No explosions. No defeat. Only the heartbreaking realization that healing takes more than one good moment.

## Emotional Objective

**Primary:** Sadness  
**Secondary:** Reflection, empathy, quiet determination

Children should think: **I wish I could help.**  
Adults should think: **Repair takes practice.**

## Camera Language

- **Lens:** 35 mm
- **Camera position:** High overhead, nearly straight down
- **Composition role:** First overhead composition in the book
- **Scale:** Characters appear small within Maplewood Terrace
- **Subject:** The neighborhood itself becomes the subject

This sharply contrasts with the intimate portrait of Beat 8 and the telephoto observation of Beat 9.

## Composition

The Sparkle Grid forms subtle branching patterns beneath the brothers. Bubble Boy and Captain Cash stand together in the lower-left quadrant. Dad and Mom are farther away, giving the boys room to wrestle with the problem.

The Echo Ogre occupies the upper-right quadrant, integrated into the landscape rather than towering over it.

The Community Mural, Solar Baobab, Floating Garden, and Innovation Plaza should create a recognizable map of Maplewood Terrace. Large areas of negative space emphasize emotional isolation.

## Visual Hierarchy

1. Bubble Boy and Captain Cash standing quietly together
2. The neighborhood losing harmony
3. The Echo Ogre becoming heavier, not larger

## Character Acting

**Bubble Boy:** Orange suit matching `BB_Model_v06.png`. Shoulders slightly slumped. One hand remains near Captain Cash. He looks toward the fading Sparkle Grid rather than the Ogre. His expression is compassion mixed with uncertainty.

**Captain Cash:** Green suit matching `CC_Model_v05.png`. No superhero stance. He quietly reaches for Bubble Boy's hand. His expression says, "I don't know what to do."

**Dad:** Watches from a respectful distance and allows the boys to arrive at the lesson themselves. Expression: patient confidence.

**Mom:** Notices the neighborhood before anyone else. One hand gently touches a dimmed flower. Expression: hopeful concern.

**Echo Ogre:** Matches `EO_ConstructionGuide_v02.png` and `EO_Stage06_Heavy.png`. He is unchanged in height but feels denser, burdened by accumulated echoes. He remains translucent, with the neighborhood visible through him.

The Echo Ogre has accumulated:

- Additional mural fragments
- Repeated gestures
- Broken sound-wave ribbons
- Incomplete story pages
- Duplicated geometric motifs

His spiral chest glows more dimly. He watches the brothers with sadness rather than menace. His lower body remains rooted in Sparkle Grid pathways, flowering vines, woven community geometry, and mural fragments.

## Environment Response

Keep the changes subtle.

- The mural loses another small piece of its story
- A Floating Garden descends a little farther
- One musical wind sculpture falls silent
- A single pathway segment fades
- The reopened flower from Beat 8 begins to droop again but does not close completely

The world is asking for more repair.

## Lighting

Late afternoon. Warm sunlight is softer and lower. Golden light still exists but no longer reaches every corner. Cool violet shadows spread gently through the plaza.

No darkness. Only imbalance.

## Color Script

- Warm gold: 50%
- Natural greens: 20%
- Turquoise: 10%
- Violet: 20%

The color balance reflects a community suspended between healing and decline.

## Sparkle Grid State

**Health:** 55%  
**State:** Harmony continues to weaken. The Grid has not failed. It is waiting.

## Hidden Storytelling

Reward careful readers with five discoveries:

- A child quietly returns a dropped book to another child in the distance
- One tiny Sparkle Grid node brightens in response
- A pollinator drone chooses a real flower over its programmed route
- One mural figure reaches toward another, but the image remains incomplete
- Inside the Echo Ogre's chest vortex, a reflected shard shows Dad kneeling between arguing neighbors from Spread 2

## Visual Density

Approximately 20%. This should be one of the quietest spreads in the book. The empty space is part of the storytelling.

## Print Specifications

- Full spread: 17.25 x 8.75 inches
- Trim: 8.5 x 8.5 inches with bleed
- Resolution: 300 DPI minimum
- Color: CMYK safe
- Reserve generous negative space in the upper sky for typography
- Keep principal characters outside the center gutter
- Ensure the overhead composition remains legible at thumbnail size

## Art Direction Summary

This spread should feel profoundly different from every previous one. By shifting to a high overhead viewpoint, the reader sees Maplewood Terrace almost like a living organism, with the brothers appearing small within its interconnected systems.

The focus is not defeating the Echo Ogre. The focus is recognizing that true repair requires sustained relationships throughout the community. The Echo Ogre is heavier with accumulated echoes but remains rooted in the neighborhood, reinforcing that he is not an invader. He is a manifestation of the world's forgotten harmony.

The final emotional impression should be quiet sadness paired with the conviction that healing is still possible.
